Our History

Halloween originated from the Celtic festival of Samhain, celebrated by ancient Irish and Scottish people to mark the end of summer and the beginning of the dark half of the year.

They believed that on the night before November 1, the boundary between the world of the living and the dead blurred, allowing spirits to return to Earth. To ward off evil spirits, people dressed in frightening costumes.

Over time, the holiday evolved, and its name became a shortened form of "All Hallows' Eve," eventually turning into Halloween.

Modern Halloween blends old pagan and Christian traditions and became especially popular in the United States, where customs like trick-or-treating and carving pumpkins developed.
